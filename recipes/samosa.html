Samosa

Description

Samosa is a popular Indian snack featuring a crispy, golden pastry filled with a savory mix of spiced potatoes, peas, and sometimes meat.
Triangular in shape and usually deep-fried, samosas are enjoyed hot with tangy chutneys, making them a favorite street food and party appetizer across South Asia.
Their flaky crust and flavorful filling offer a delightful contrast in every bite. Loved for their versatility, samosas are perfect for tea-time, festive occasions, or as a satisfying snack anytime.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 4 tablespoons oil (plus more for deep frying)
  • 6 tablespoons water (or as needed for dough)
  • 3–4 medium potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • 1/2 cup green peas (boiled or frozen)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger (minced or finely chopped)
  • 1–2 green chilies (chopped, optional)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder) or lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon fennel powder (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon hing (asafoetida, optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves (cilantro)
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix 2 cups all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon carom seeds, and 1/4 teaspoon salt. Add 4 tablespoons oil and rub it into the flour until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  2. Gradually add about 6 tablespoons water, kneading until a firm, smooth dough forms. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  3. For the filling, heat 2 tablespoons oil in a pan. Add 1 teaspoon cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon ginger, 1–2 chopped green chilies (optional), and a pinch of hing if using. Sauté for a minute.
  5. Add 3–4 boiled and mashed potatoes and 1/2 cup green peas. Stir well.
  6. Mix in 1 teaspoon garam masala, 1 teaspoon chili powder, 1/2 teaspoon amchur or lemon juice, 1/2 teaspoon fennel powder (optional), and salt to taste. Cook for 2–3 minutes.
  7. Add 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves and mix. Let the filling cool completely.
  8. Divide the dough into equal balls. Roll each into an oval, cut in half to form two semi-circles.
  9. Shape each semi-circle into a cone, sealing the edge with water. Fill the cone with potato mixture, then seal the top edge to close the samosa.
  10. Heat oil for deep frying. Fry samosas on medium heat until golden and crisp. Drain on paper towels and serve hot with chutney.
